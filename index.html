Although he was a trained musician, Fela was a notorious political activist who shunned western civilisation and was always challenging the etablishemnt through his music.

    Here are some facts about Fela's life

  • Fela Kuti was born on October 15, 1938, in Abeokuta, Nigeria, West Africa
  • Fela Kuti was named Olufela Olusegun Oludotun Ransome-Kuti at birth.
  • He later changed his name to Olufela Anikulapo Kuti, thus removing the English name 'Ransome' from his name
  • His mother, Funmilayo, was also a political activist, who was the first female to drive a car in Nigeria
  • Fela pioneered his own unique style of music called "Afrobeat."
  • Afrobeat is a combination of funk, jazz, salsa, Calypso and traditional Nigerian Yoruba music.
  • Fela contacted HIV and later died of AIDS-related complications on August 2, 1997, at the age of 58, in Lagos, Nigeria.
  • Approximately, over 1 million people attended his funeral procession, in Lagos Nigeria which brought the whole city to a standtill
